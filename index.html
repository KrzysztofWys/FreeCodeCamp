Marek Kotański 1942- 2002

I sold myself to people

Selected dates from the life of Marek Kotański

  • 1942 - Marek Kotański was born in Warsaw. His mother was a painter- Ludwika, and a father was a Professor of Japanese Studies - Wiesław Kotański.
  • 1960 - Starting psychology studies at the Warsaw. During his studies, he was active in the Ruch Młodych Wychowawców (Movement of Young Educators) who looked after orphans and young people affected by social problems. After his studies, he worked as a therapist in the psychiatric hospital.
  • 1974 - Marek Kotański was employed in the Psychiatric Hospital in Garwolin, which had a section for drug addicts. As the traditional methods were not effective, he started a therapy group. This sort of therapy group came to be known as "społeczność" (community).
  • 1978 - The first Monar centre was opened on 15 October 1978 in Gloskow near Garwolin, starting in part of an abandoned house in ruins. Kotański started this program with a group or patients from the hospital in Garwolin. The results were better than expected. Now there are more than 157 Monar centres in Poland. In 80's he organized also settlements for people with HIV or ill with AIDS
  • 1993 Kotański created Markot - Ruch Wychodzenia z Bezdomności (Movement Out of Homelessness), which involves about one hundred centres for homeless people, lone mothers with children, handicapped people, and the terminally ill.
  • 1994 He also developed a help system for people coming out of prison, starting from 1994, without any financial support.
  • 1996 Marek KOtański was prized with Order of Polonia Restituta, the second most important order in Poland.
  • 2002 August 19th Marek Kotaski died in a hospital, after a car accident near Warsaw.

Commemoration

  • Since 2002 - there are organized yearly charity concerts "Daj siebie innym - Dzien Kotana" (Give Yourself to the Others -Kotan Day).
  • In 2006 in Ozorków was created a soccer club named "Kotan", with a Marek Kotański profile imagein a club crest.
